Denali National Park protects six million acres of subarctic wilderness. Private vehicles are limited beyond Mile 15 on the park road, so most visitors explore by narrated bus tour, transit bus or guided excursion.

Park Road Access

The 92-mile park road winds through tundra, river valleys and mountain passes. Tour buses reach deeper sections where wildlife sightings are most common.

What To Expect

Weather changes quickly. Dress in layers, bring binoculars and plan for a full day when booking a deep-park tour. Denali peak is visible only on clear days.

Season

Main visitor season runs mid-May through mid-September when park road transit operates fully.

Wildlife

The park's Big Five — moose, caribou, Dall sheep, wolves and bears — are seen regularly on bus routes.

Booking

Reserve park tours and lodges early for peak summer weeks, especially for cruise-linked travel dates.
